What is information physically made out of?

ChatGPT

Information, in a physical sense, is typically considered to be a pattern or arrangement of matter and energy that can be interpreted to convey meaning. In digital systems, information is encoded in bits, which are represented by physical states of a medium, such as the magnetic orientation of particles on a hard drive, the electrical charge in a semiconductor, or the polarization of light in optical fibers.

More broadly, information can be seen as a concept that is abstract but has physical representations. For example:

1. Classical Information Theory: Information is often represented by the state of a physical system, such as the arrangement of atoms in a printed page or the voltage levels in a computer circuit.

2. Quantum Information Theory: Information is represented by quantum states, which can be in superpositions of multiple states simultaneously.

Thus, while information itself is an abstract concept, it always requires a physical substrate to be stored, processed, and transmitted.
